## Matters

Matters are workspaces for organizing your legal work by case, deal, or project. Each Matter keeps documents, conversations, and research isolated so information doesn't leak between engagements.

### Creating a Matter

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Matters">
    Navigate to the Matters section from the sidebar in your [dashboard](https://app.vaquill.ai).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new Matter">
    Click **New Matter** and give it a name. Optionally link it to a client.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add documents">
    Upload documents directly into the Matter. All documents within a Matter are searchable together.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start conversations">
    Conversations within a Matter automatically have access to all its documents.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Matter Features

- **Document isolation:** Documents in one Matter are not visible to conversations in another
- **Client linking:** Associate Matters with clients for organization
- **Conversation history:** All conversations within a Matter are preserved and searchable
- **Export:** Export Matter contents including conversations and analysis results

## Client Management

Group related Matters under Clients for a cleaner workspace:

- Create client profiles with basic information
- Link multiple Matters to a single client
- View all work for a client in one place

## Team Collaboration

Vaquill supports team workspaces with role-based access:

| Role | Permissions |
|------|------------|
| **Owner** | Full access, billing, team management |
| **Admin** | Manage members, create/delete Matters |
| **Member** | Create conversations, upload documents, use tools |
| **Viewer** | Read-only access to shared conversations and documents |

### Sharing Conversations

Share specific conversations with team members or generate a public share link for external collaboration. Shared conversations include the full thread with all citations and tool outputs.

### Inline Threading

Start focused sub-conversations within a main thread using inline threading. This keeps detailed discussions organized without cluttering the primary conversation.
